January: Dry Eyes

Cold air, wind, and indoor heat can strip moisture from the eye surface. Increase hydration, use preservative-free tears, and consider a humidifier at night.

February: Indoor Eye Strain

Long screen hours in darker months can trigger digital fatigue. Follow the 20-20-20 rule, blink fully, and adjust monitor brightness to match room lighting.

March: Early Allergies

As early allergens rise, itchy and watery eyes become common. Start allergy eye drops early, avoid rubbing, and rinse lids after outdoor time.

April: Tree Pollen

Tree pollen peaks can inflame sensitive eyes. Keep windows closed on high-count days, shower after being outside, and use cold compresses for relief.

May: UV Protection

Longer, brighter days mean more UV exposure. Wear quality sunglasses with UV400 protection and a brimmed hat during midday activities.

June: Chlorine and Irritation

Pool season can irritate eyes due to chlorine and debris. Wear swim goggles, rinse eyes after swimming, and avoid sleeping in contact lenses.

July: Summer Dryness and Dehydration

Heat, wind, and air conditioning can worsen dryness. Keep water intake up, use lubricating drops before outdoor activity, and take shade breaks.

August: Travel and Contact Lens Hygiene

Travel routines can lead to shortcut lens habits. Pack backup glasses, never top off old lens solution, and wash hands before lens handling.

September: Back-to-School Screen Time

Study demands and device use can increase eye fatigue. Encourage frequent visual breaks, proper reading distance, and annual pediatric eye checks.

October: Fall Allergens and Air Quality

Ragweed and fluctuating air quality can flare irritation. Use wraparound eyewear outdoors and maintain clean home air filters as seasons shift.

November: Early Darkness and Glare

Lower sun angles and dark commutes can intensify glare. Keep windshields clean, update lens coatings as needed, and report night-vision changes promptly.

December: Dry Eye and Age Concerns

Winter dryness plus age-related changes can impact comfort and clarity. Schedule year-end exams with Cedarbrae Eye Doctors, review medications, and discuss cataract or macular risk factors.
